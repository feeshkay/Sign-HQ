NAD/RID Code of Professional Conduct
Seven Tenets · Effective Standard of Practice
TENET 1
Confidentiality
Interpreters hold information received during interpreting assignments confidential. This includes names, personal or professional information, and any information that is part of the assignment context. Interpreters do not reveal private information shared during assignments to anyone who is not a direct party in the communication.
TENET 2
Professional Skills & Knowledge
Interpreters accept assignments only when they have the requisite skills and knowledge to work effectively, including appropriate language competence in both languages. Interpreters assess their own skills and take responsibility for self-improvement through ongoing professional development.
TENET 3
Conduct
Interpreters are professionals who bring a set of skills and a code of professional ethics to every assignment. Interpreters behave professionally in demeanor and appearance, and adhere to the ethical practices of the profession as described in this code.
TENET 4
Respect for Consumers
Interpreters are committed to equitable communication access for all parties. Interpreters treat all consumers with respect, impartiality, and professional consideration regardless of race, color, national origin, gender, sexual orientation, religion, age, or disability.
TENET 5
Respect for Colleagues
Interpreters are members of a professional community. Interpreters treat colleagues with collegiality and professionalism. When working as a team, interpreters support their partners and do not engage in behavior that discredits, dishonors, or denigrates their colleagues.
TENET 6
Ethical Business Practices
Interpreters engage in fair and honest business practices. Interpreters accurately represent their skills, education, certifications, and experience. Interpreters set rates based on their professional skills, locale, and market conditions. Interpreters honor commitments made to referral agencies, direct clients, and consumers.
TENET 7
Professional Development
Interpreters continue their professional development, maintain competence through ongoing education, and take advantage of opportunities to advance their knowledge and skills. Interpreters are committed to providing the highest quality of service through lifelong learning.
Performing Arts Addendum
Team rotation: When co-interpreting, agree on rotation schedule before the assignment. Support your partner and communicate needs clearly.
Prep obligation: Obtain and review scripts, lyrics, and scores in advance. Work with directors to understand artistic intent and Deaf cultural representation goals.
Cultural consultation: For productions involving culturally-specific content, consult with Deaf community members for authentic representation.
Impartiality: Interpreters do not counsel, advise, or interject personal opinions. Maintain the speaker's intent and register throughout.
