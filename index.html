NAD/RID Code of Professional Conduct
Seven Tenets · Effective Standard of Practice
TENET 1
Confidentiality
Interpreters hold information received during interpreting assignments confidential. This includes names, personal or professional information, and any information that is part of the assignment context.
TENET 2
Professional Skills & Knowledge
Interpreters accept assignments only when they have the requisite skills and knowledge to work effectively, including appropriate language competence in both languages. Interpreters assess their own skills and take responsibility for self-improvement.
TENET 3
Conduct
Interpreters are professionals who bring a set of skills and a code of professional ethics to every assignment. Interpreters behave professionally in demeanor and appearance.
TENET 4
Respect for Consumers
Interpreters are committed to equitable communication access for all parties. Interpreters treat all consumers with respect, impartiality, and professional consideration regardless of race, color, national origin, gender, sexual orientation, religion, age, or disability.
TENET 5
Respect for Colleagues
Interpreters treat colleagues with collegiality and professionalism. When team interpreting, interpreters support their partners and do not engage in behavior that discredits or denigrates colleagues.
TENET 6
Ethical Business Practices
Interpreters engage in fair and honest business practices. Interpreters accurately represent their skills, education, certifications, and experience. Interpreters honor commitments made to referral agencies, direct clients, and consumers.
TENET 7
Professional Development
Interpreters continue their professional development and maintain competence through ongoing education. Interpreters are committed to providing the highest quality of service through lifelong learning.
Performing Arts Addendum
Prep obligation: Obtain and review scripts, lyrics, and scores in advance. Work with directors to understand artistic intent and Deaf cultural representation goals.
Team rotation: Agree on rotation schedule before the assignment. Support your partner; communicate needs clearly and without judgment.
Cultural consultation: For culturally-specific content, consult with Deaf community members for authentic representation.
